It reminds me he turned Anglican from Evangelical and one of his major points was coming to inhabit that the Bible, which he loves and is a genuine master of, is not the _source_ of faith: it's a product of faith, the faith of those who walked with Moses and David and Hezekiah and Josiah and Daniel and Jeremiah and Isaiah and Ezra and Nehemiah and Jesus, whose actions changed the world. The Writings are a very important part, but indeed "only" a part, of the truth, which is that walking.
